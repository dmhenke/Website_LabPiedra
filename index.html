Who We Are

Laboratory Overview

The Piedra Laboratory, situated within the Department of Molecular Virology and Microbiology at Baylor College of Medicine, has been at the forefront of respiratory virus research since 1987. Under the direction of Dr. Pedro A. "Tony" Piedra, M.D., the lab pursues a singular mission: reducing the burden of respiratory viral illness — particularly respiratory syncytial virus (RSV) and emerging pandemic threats — through rigorous basic, translational, and clinical investigation. The laboratory is embedded in the Texas Medical Center and maintains deep collaborative ties with Texas Children's Hospital and the CDC-funded New Vaccine Surveillance Network (NVSN).

Our integrated research program spans ex vivo organoid modeling, whole-genome viral surveillance, immunology of natural infection and vaccination, and clinical trials of novel therapeutics and preventive interventions. Dr. Piedra additionally directs Baylor's CLIA/FDA-Certified Respiratory Virus Diagnostic Laboratory and leads the BCM Pandemic Threat Technology Center (PTTC), positioning the lab at the nexus of discovery science and pandemic preparedness.

RSV Pathogenesis & Immunity

We investigate the molecular and cellular mechanisms underlying RSV infection across age groups, including why infants experience more severe disease than adults. Our human nose organoid (HNO) air-liquid interface platform provides a physiologically relevant, non-invasive model to dissect innate immune responses, viral tropism, and mucociliary dysfunction.

Vaccine & Prophylaxis Development

The laboratory has contributed to the clinical evaluation of maternal RSV vaccines, long-acting monoclonal antibodies, and subunit vaccine candidates. We serve as a principal Baylor site for phase II–III multi-center trials and perform immunogenicity and efficacy assessments including serology and viral challenge studies.

Respiratory Virus Surveillance

Through active participation in the CDC's New Vaccine Surveillance Network, we conduct community-based epidemiological studies tracking RSV, influenza, SARS-CoV-2, and other respiratory pathogens across pediatric and adult populations in Houston and nationally, directly informing public health policy.

Genomic & Evolutionary Virology

Leveraging whole-genome sequencing, probe-based capture enrichment, and single-cell transcriptomics, we characterize RSV and SARS-CoV-2 evolutionary dynamics across pandemic and interpandemic periods. During COVID-19, the lab processed up to 350 samples daily with 24-hour turnaround, tracking viral mutations in real time to inform vaccine strain coverage decisions.

CLIA Diagnostic Laboratory

Dr. Piedra directs Baylor's FDA-Certified Respiratory Virus Diagnostic Laboratory, which supports clinical care, research, and public health surveillance. The lab receives samples from Baylor's affiliated hospitals and played a central role in large-scale community testing and viral sequencing during the COVID-19 pandemic in the Houston metropolitan area.

Pediatric & Maternal Immunization

We study antibody kinetics, durability of protection, the role of respiratory viruses in febrile infants under 90 days of age, and original antigenic sin phenomena. Research areas include bronchiolitis and its relation to asthma, biomarkers of acute respiratory viral disease, and clinical significance of respiratory infections in pregnant women.

BCM Pandemic Threat Technology Center (PTTC)

Dr. Piedra serves as founding Director of the BCM Pandemic Threat Technology Center, which leads and coordinates research and responses to infectious diseases posing local, regional, and global pandemic threats. The PTTC maintains cutting-edge molecular diagnostic and assay development platforms, and proof-of-concept clinical study infrastructure across adult and pediatric populations. Real-time community virus monitoring data is publicly available through the PTTC.

Our People

Laboratory Members

The Piedra Lab is a multidisciplinary team of physician-scientists, research faculty, postdoctoral fellows, graduate students, and research staff committed to translational virology. Dr. Piedra has mentored graduate students, postdoctoral fellows, and physicians in basic and translational research for over 30 years. We actively welcome trainees from diverse scientific backgrounds.

Principal Investigator

PAP

Pedro A. "Tony" Piedra, M.D.

Professor · Molecular Virology & Microbiology · Pediatrics · Pharmacology & Chemical Biology

Dr. Piedra is a Cuban-born physician-scientist who trained at the University of Florida (B.S., 1978) and the University of South Florida College of Medicine (M.D., 1981), completed a Pediatrics residency at the Medical University of South Carolina (1984), and a Pediatric Infectious Disease fellowship at the Children's Hospital of Buffalo (1987). He joined Baylor College of Medicine in 1987, and his current research effort is entirely devoted to translational respiratory virus science — he does not maintain an active clinical practice.

His research spans molecular epidemiology of RSV, immune correlates of RSV disease, viral surveillance of moderate-to-severe acute respiratory illness in children, the role of respiratory viruses in febrile infants under 90 days of age, clinical significance of respiratory viral infections in pregnant women, bronchiolitis and asthma relationships, and prevention of RSV through vaccine and drug development. Dr. Piedra also serves as Director of Baylor's CLIA/FDA-Certified Respiratory Virus Diagnostic Laboratory and as founding Director of the BCM Pandemic Threat Technology Center. He is a member of the Gulf Coast Consortia Immunology Cluster.

In the News

The Piedra Laboratory's research and expertise have been featured in academic, public health, and popular media, reflecting the broad translational and societal impact of our work on respiratory viral disease prevention and pandemic response.

BCM Research News

June 2025

For the First Time, Safe and Effective RSV Prevention Treatments Significantly Reduce Infant Hospitalizations

Dr. Piedra and co-investigators reported findings from a nationwide study of nearly 20,000 infants demonstrating that maternal RSV vaccination and long-acting monoclonal antibody prophylaxis reduce RSV-related hospitalizations by at least 50% in infants aged 0–2 months — the highest-risk age group.

BCM News · bcm.edu · June 2025
EurekAlert / Journal of Infection

October 2025

Human 'Mini-Noses' Help Understand Why RSV Infections Are More Severe in Children

Single-cell transcriptomic analyses of pediatric versus adult human nose organoids revealed infant-specific cell types, accelerated epithelial proliferation, and a dysregulated innate immune response that collectively explain the heightened severity of RSV in young children.

EurekAlert · BCM From the Labs · October 2025
KPRC / Click2Houston Investigates

July 2020

Houston Researchers Monitor Virus Mutations to Fight COVID-19 Pandemic

KPRC 2 Houston featured Dr. Piedra and his team — including Dr. Avadhanula, Dr. Rajan, and Dr. Ye — describing how the lab pivoted its RSV research infrastructure to process up to 350 COVID-19 samples per day with 24-hour turnaround, tracking viral mutations and using organoid models to study how the virus attacks human airway tissue.

KPRC Click2Houston · July 9, 2020

Model of the Human Nose Reveals First Steps of SARS-CoV-2 and RSV Infection

The Piedra Lab's non-invasive human nose organoid (HNO) system — published in mBio — enabled the first ex vivo comparisons of RSV and SARS-CoV-2 pathogenesis in physiologically relevant airway epithelium, garnering wide scientific press coverage.
