Who We Are

Laboratory Overview

The Piedra Laboratory, situated within the Department of Molecular Virology and Microbiology at Baylor College of Medicine, has been at the forefront of respiratory virus research since 1987. Under the direction of Dr. Pedro A. "Tony" Piedra, M.D., the lab pursues a singular mission: reducing the burden of respiratory viral illness — particularly RSV and emerging pandemic threats — through rigorous basic, translational, and clinical investigation. The laboratory maintains deep collaborative ties with Texas Children's Hospital and the CDC-funded New Vaccine Surveillance Network (NVSN), and Dr. Piedra additionally directs the BCM Pandemic Threat Technology Center (PTTC).

🔬

RSV Pathogenesis & Immunity

Molecular and cellular mechanisms of RSV infection across age groups, using human nose organoid (HNO) air-liquid interface platforms to dissect innate immunity, viral tropism, and mucociliary dysfunction.

💉

Vaccine & Prophylaxis Development

Clinical evaluation of maternal RSV vaccines, long-acting monoclonal antibodies, and subunit vaccine candidates. Site PI for phase II–III trials; immunogenicity and viral challenge study design.

📊

Respiratory Virus Surveillance

CDC New Vaccine Surveillance Network (NVSN) community-based epidemiology across pediatric and adult populations in Houston — directly informing national public health policy.

🧬

Genomic & Evolutionary Virology

Whole-genome sequencing and single-cell transcriptomics to characterize RSV and SARS-CoV-2 evolutionary dynamics. During COVID-19, the lab processed 350 samples/day tracking viral mutations in real time.

🏥

CLIA Diagnostic Laboratory

Dr. Piedra directs Baylor's FDA-Certified Respiratory Virus Diagnostic Laboratory, serving affiliated hospitals and playing a central role in large-scale COVID-19 testing and sequencing in Houston.

🤱

Pediatric & Maternal Immunization

Antibody kinetics, durability of protection, febrile infants under 90 days, respiratory infections in pregnancy, bronchiolitis-asthma relationships, and biomarkers of acute respiratory viral disease.

BCM Research Center

Pandemic Threat Technology Center (PTTC)

Dr. Piedra serves as founding Director of the BCM PTTC, which leads and coordinates research and responses to infectious diseases posing local, regional, and global pandemic threats. The Center maintains cutting-edge molecular diagnostic platforms and proof-of-concept clinical study infrastructure. Real-time community virus monitoring data are publicly accessible through the PTTC dashboard.

Our People

Laboratory Members

The Piedra Lab is a multidisciplinary team of physician-scientists, research faculty, postdoctoral fellows, graduate students, and research staff. Dr. Piedra has mentored trainees in basic and translational research for over 30 years. We welcome inquiries from prospective trainees.

Principal Investigator

Pedro A. "Tony" Piedra, M.D.

Professor · Molecular Virology & Microbiology · Pediatrics · Pharmacology & Chemical Biology

Dr. Piedra is a physician-scientist who trained at the University of Florida (B.S., 1978) and the University of South Florida College of Medicine (M.D., 1981), completed a Pediatrics residency at the Medical University of South Carolina (1984), and a Pediatric Infectious Disease fellowship at the Children's Hospital of Buffalo (1987). He joined Baylor College of Medicine in 1987, devoting his full effort to translational respiratory virus science.

His research spans molecular epidemiology of RSV, immune correlates of RSV disease, viral surveillance in children, respiratory viruses in febrile infants under 90 days, infections in pregnant women, bronchiolitis–asthma relationships, and RSV prevention. He directs both Baylor's CLIA/FDA-Certified Respiratory Virus Diagnostic Laboratory and the BCM Pandemic Threat Technology Center, and is a member of the Gulf Coast Consortia Immunology Cluster.

Media & Outreach

In the News

The Piedra Laboratory's research and expertise have been featured across academic, public health, and media outlets, reflecting the translational and societal impact of our work on respiratory viral disease prevention and pandemic response.

BCM Research News

June 2025

RSV Prevention Treatments Significantly Reduce Infant Hospitalizations for the First Time

A nationwide study of nearly 20,000 infants showed maternal RSV vaccination and long-acting monoclonal antibody prophylaxis reduce RSV-related hospitalizations by at least 50% in infants aged 0–2 months — the highest-risk group.

EurekAlert / Journal of Infection

October 2025

Human 'Mini-Noses' Help Explain Why RSV Infections Are More Severe in Children

Single-cell transcriptomic analyses of pediatric versus adult human nose organoids revealed infant-specific cell types and dysregulated innate immunity explaining heightened RSV severity in young children.

KPRC / Click2Houston Investigates

July 2020

Houston Researchers Monitor Virus Mutations to Fight COVID-19 Pandemic

An investigative TV report featured Dr. Piedra, Dr. Avadhanula, Dr. Rajan, and Dr. Ye — describing how the lab pivoted its RSV infrastructure to process 350 COVID-19 samples/day with 24-hour turnaround, tracking mutations in real time using organoid models and antibody assays.

ScienceDaily / mBio

February 2022

Human Nose Organoid Model Reveals First Steps of SARS-CoV-2 and RSV Infection

The Piedra Lab's non-invasive HNO system enabled the first ex vivo comparative study of RSV and SARS-CoV-2 pathogenesis in physiologically relevant airway epithelium, attracting extensive scientific press coverage.

EurekAlert / Vaccine

February 2021

RSV Antibody Response Varies Among Adults, Revealing a Susceptible Subpopulation

A Piedra Lab study revealing that a subset of adults fail to sustain RSV antibodies after natural reinfection — with direct implications for COVID-19 vaccine design — was widely syndicated through scientific media.

BCM From the Labs · Hispanic Heritage

December 2020

Interview with Dr. Pedro A. Piedra: Physician-Scientist, Mentor, Community Voice

BCM recognized Dr. Piedra during Hispanic Heritage Month, covering his journey from Cuba and Panama to leading BCM's respiratory virus research program and his COVID-19 pandemic response leadership.
